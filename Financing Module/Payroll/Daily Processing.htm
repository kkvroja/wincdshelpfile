Payroll Weekly Processing

Note: Always use Arial fonts on all reports.

Main Payroll Help can be found in the Payroll Module.

Note: Before you begin the weekly payroll, be sure to enter any NEW employees before you begin the payroll.

  1. Open the payroll program.  If you are multiple stores and have a separate checking account for Each store, make sure you pick the correct location to log into. 

  2. Click on Processing  button. 

  1.  

  2. From the blank form which appears, enter the Check Date, Period Start, and Period End dates.

  3. For each employee, enter the Department number the employee was primarily in.  select the employee name from the drop down.

  4. This employee worked 48 hours including 8 hours of overtime.  Notice that in Shift 1, we entered 48 hours.  The software automatically filled the Overtime 1 with 8 hours. 

  5. Click on the Add button.  This set the transaction into the database.

  6. Continue with all the employees.  Note: Salary employees get automatically filled at 40 hours.  This is correct.

  7. On the final employee, click OK.  This puts you back to the main menu.

  8. Now that all the employees are entered, click on Processing; Calculate Payroll button.  Then clik process Payroll. This actually adds Taxes and all deductions.

  9. EXTREMELY IMPORTANT!  Make a Payroll back Up NOW!  If you have a printer jam, or something goes wrong you will not have to void a lot of payroll checks that cannot be used again even if they are blank.

  10. After you review the printed checks for deductions and earnings, click on Reports, Check Register and print out the check register for the checks just printed. 

  11. If we are at the end of a quarter, click on Reports, and print out the 941, F.U.T., S.U.T. reports.  At year end, you can even print out W-2's from the software. 

Note: The process of printing W-2's can be done at any time after the end of the calendar year. The data that is used to generate the W-2's is the payroll transactions that made up all the checks through the year. This information is never deleted, so you don't have to rush to print W-2's between the last payroll for a year and the first payroll for the new year.
