Payment and View

Here is the account Payment and View screen.

There are seven parts to this screen:

  1. Customer and Account Information
  2. Terms and Conditions
  3. Balance and Payments
  4. Ageing and Commands
  5. Method of Payment
  6. Move Records
  7. Letter To Customer


The sections are explained below:

1 - Customer and Account Information

This sections contains the information about the customer, as well as the account status. At the top is the account number as well as the status indicating whether this account is Open, Closed, Void, Write off, or another status. You cannot change anything in this box while the form is in Payment and View mode. To be able to change the status, you must go to the Edit Accounts screen.
The status will automatically change to Closed when the account is paid off or Void is the contract is voided.

Additionally, you can click on View Cust. History in the lower right of this box to see all transactions from all Ar Accounts from this customer.

2- Terms and Conditions

This sections displays the original terms of the contract. This information is only editable under the Edit Accounts screen. It shows the original delivery date, date of first last payment, as well as original financing options and late fees.

From this box, you can reprint contract information for the customer or your file. You can click:
  • Reprint Contract - Reprint the installment contract.
  • Reprint Coupons - Reprint the customer's coupon book.




Payment Summary Box:



In the Orange box, you can view the payment summary for this customer. Each character represents one month of customer history. The first character represents the account payment status at the date shown directly above the box (calculated to be the most recent late date adjusted for Grace period). The next character represents the account status for the previous month, one month prior to the date shown. The third character is two months ago.

The box is formatted to show 12 months per line, so the start of the second line will show one year prior, followed by one year and one month, followed by one year and two months, and so on.

The individual characters are interpreted as follows:

1Account was current
2Account was 1 payment behind
3Account was 2 payments behind
4Account was 3 payments behind
5Account was 4 or more payments behind
BlankNo payment history for this month (account was likely not open then).

Things to Note:

From the above example, notice that as the customer misses payments (e.g., March & April), the current number (the left-most one) increase, but it also shows the trend. On 4/21, for example, the code would show "3211", meaning, while they are 2 months behind now, they were one month behind the previous payment, and made the first two payments successfully.

Paying off the balance lowers the numbers. For example on 11/21, the code goes from 3211... in October, to 13211... This means they are now current, having paid the two previous months off, as well as the current. This is in contrast to the partial payoff on 5/21, where only paid the current month and one previous month. In that instance, the code was "23211" indicating an improvement, but still in arrears.

From this simple status indicator, you can quickly determine the health of any given contract, see patterns of delinquency, and decide how to repond accordingly.

3 - Balance and Payments

The box in the middle on the left side shows the current Balance, any Late Charges that have not been paid, and how much the customer must pay for a total Payoff at that moment.

5 - Method of Payment

Click on the appropriate option to select your method of payment.



6 - Move Records

You can use the left and right arrows to move between records. Use the double arrows to proceed to the first/last entry. You can also check or clear the Send All Mail checkbox here. To print a mailing label for this customer based in the information in box 1, select your label type and click Print Mailing Label.



7 - Letter to Customer

Here you can send customized letters to your customer. These letters must be pre-defined and they will be listed in the box. You have been given a letter of instructions on how to set up a custom letter with the original software kit. To print a letter, select it in the list and click Print. You can also Edit to edit the letter. If you want to open the letter in Word to edit before sending, you can click Export instead of printing it. To print a list of fields that are available when creating new letters, click Fields. This is a help feature for creating new letters. It will help you create or modify existing letters.
